Abstract

As Large Language Models (LLMs) are increasingly employed in high-stakes medical applications, ensuring their reliability becomes critical. Medical Hallucination Detection presents a comprehensive benchmark for evaluating and detecting hallucinated outputs in LLMs. Our study introduces a dataset and evaluation framework that distinguishes between accurate and hallucinated responses, providing insights into model performance in the medical domain.

Introduction

The deployment of LLMs in clinical settings demands rigorous validation of their factual accuracy. Hallucinations in medical responses can lead to adverse clinical outcomes. In this work, we propose a benchmark specifically tailored to detect hallucinated outputs in medical LLMs and to understand the underlying challenges in model evaluation.

Methodology

Our approach involves a multi-stage process including candidate generation, LLM-based quality filtering, and correctness verification using bidirectional entailment. By incorporating domain-specific knowledge, our framework robustly distinguishes between correct and hallucinated responses.
